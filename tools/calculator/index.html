The formulas (nothing hidden)

recompute $/hit = prefix_tokens ÷ prefill_tok_s × (gpu_$/hr ÷ 3600)

serve $/hit = prefix_tokens × bytes/token ÷ 10⁹ × transfer_$/GB

tax/month = req/day × hit_rate × recompute_$/hit × 30 — the GPU seconds you pay for again whenever a hit is not served from cache.

B* (GB/s) = bytes/token × prefill_tok_s ÷ 10⁹ — sizes in binary MiB, bandwidth/transfer in decimal GB, exactly as economics.py documents.

Provider-scale sanity check: DeepSeek's API prices cached-prefix hits roughly 50–120× cheaper than misses — ~50× on V4-Flash, up to ~120× on V4-Pro, per DeepSeek's published KV-cache docs (api-docs.deepseek.com/guides/kv_cache) and pricing page (api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing), as of Jul 2026. A vendor/provider-claimed figure, not our measurement, but it is the same hit/miss cost gap this page models.

Formula sources (verified 2026-07, see bench/e2e/economics.py): KV formula & MLA — arxiv.org/html/2505.09343v2; break-even framing — arxiv.org/abs/2510.09665 (LMCache), arxiv.org/abs/2410.03065 (Cake); GPU $/hr — runpod.io/pricing; transfer $ — AWS on-demand (same-AZ private IP $0/GB, cross-AZ $0.01/GB each way).

Honesty notes: below ~40% hit rate the savings may not clear amortized infra cost; byte counts here are honest GQA/MLA, never the 64-head MHA miscount; latency wins assume same-AZ deployment.
